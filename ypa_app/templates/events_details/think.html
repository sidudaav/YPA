Think Physics! 2020

In Think Physics!, your goal is to write a paper that aims to make progress in solving some type issue using physics. You can propose a hypothetical solution to any problem you would like. This includes global issues or a small one in our daily lives. However, whatever solution you propose must be rigorously backed by physics or other scientific principles.

Prizes

  1. The top 3 contestants will win a Maple 2020 Student 12M Term Download, worth $100 each!
  2. More prizes coming soon!

Register

  1. Click this link to register
  2. Registration must be completed before the submission deadline.
  3. Teams consist of 1-4 members. Team members do not need to be from the same school.
  4. Every team member must be going to high school as of fall 2020 in order to participate.
  5. Only one person per team should register.
  6. After registering, you will receive a message confirming your registration within a couple days.

Tasks

  1. Write a paper that proposes a hypothetical solution to a problem.
    • Solution must be based on physics and engineering principles.
  2. The submissions will be judged on specific criteria.
    • Is your paper understandable?
    • Was physics used, and if so how significant is physics to the solution itself?
    • How elegant/creative is the solution itself?
    • Can some else build on top of your work?
    • Are correct physics principles used?

Submission

  1. Email your submission to YPAsubmission@gmail.com by September 18, 11:59:00 PM EDT.
  2. You must submit either one google doc, a word doc or a pdf.
  3. Your paper must not have more than 3000 words including everything (ex. title, author name, headers). If you have more, your paper will be penalized.
  4. Please provide an email of everyone on the team.
  5. Please have the team members' names written on the report.
  6. The due date for the submission is September 18, 11:59:00 PM EDT.

Judging

  1. There will be around 9 judges total: 7 judges for part 1 and 2 judges for part 2. The judging process will be broken into two parts.
    • PART 1: Each judge in part 1 will give a score out of 10 in each of the categories listed in Task(2)(a-d), and the 10 papers with the highest average score will be considered for part 2.
    • PART 2: Each judge in part 2 will give a score out of 10 for the category Task(2)(e), and the ranking of the top 10 will be decided by the average score given.
