Code-ify Physics! 2020

In YPA’s third contest ever, you code any physics related program you wish! This can be done in Python, Java, or any other coding languages you desire. Possibilities include educational software, simulations and demonstrations, websites, and much more! The total prizes available are worth around $1000!

Prizes

  1. The top 3 contestants will win a 1 year licenses for both Wolfram | One and Wolfram | Alpha ($300+ VALUE for each winner).
  2. More prizes may be added depending on how much interest is shown.

Register

  1. Click this link to register
  2. Registration must be completed before the submission deadline.
  3. This is an individual contest.
  4. You must be going to high school as of fall 2020 in order to participate.
  5. After registering, you will receive a message confirming your registration within a couple days.

Tasks

  1. Combine physics with computer science in an interesting way.
  2. Submit your codebase and a description of your project.
    • Any programming language may be used.
    • The description may not be longer than 600 words.
  3. The submissions will be judged on specific criteria.
    • Useability/Effectiveness: your project accurately serves its designed purpose.
    • Uniqueness/Creativity: novelty and originality of ideas are highly valued.
    • Implementation of physics: your program must show a significant combination of physics and computer science.
    • Complexity: thought and time has been put into the project.

Rules

  1. All work must be largely the contestant’s.
  2. If your program is not self explanatory, then you are expected to submit a google docs/word document/pdf explain how to use your program with max 600 words, or have a tutorial on your program.
  3. Do not have your name on your program for judging purposes.

Submission

  1. Email your submission to YPAsubmission@gmail.com by September 18, 11:59:00 PM EDT.
  2. Only email once per person, if you need to edit your submission reply to the email you sent, replies after the due date will be ignored.
  3. Make the subject line of the email → [your name] Code-ify Physics 2020.
  4. If your program contains multiple files please zip instead of giving multiple files, otherwise submit an individual file, this does not include instruction or description of the program.
  5. If your files size is beyond 25 MB (at this point google will not allow you to send the email), please upload your file to a google drive and share the link with YPAsubmission@gmail.com, and give the email permission to judge.
  6. Submit any documents or information which will make analyzing your program easier for the judges.

Judging

  1. Judging will involve around 8 judges and will be broken into two parts.
    • PART 1: each judge will give a score out of 10 to each of the qualities listed in Tasks(2), and the 10 best programs with the highest scores on average will be considered for part 2.
    • PART 2: each judge will rank the top 10, and the winners will be decided by best average rank from all of the judges. The ranking will be based off of the qualities listed in Task(2).
  2. While judging, the judges will not know the name of the contestant to remove any bias. Having your name on the program is not recommended as doing so will result in lower judge scores.
